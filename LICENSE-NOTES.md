Charitable organisations, educational institutions, public research
organisations and government bodies are explicitly permitted regardless of
funding source — that's part of the PolyForm noncommercial definition.

## Why this license and not "real" open source

The goal is:

1. Keep the code visible, forkable and improvable.
2. Prevent someone from taking Quappe, hosting it as a paid product, and
   turning the same anti-social patterns we're trying to avoid into a
   revenue stream on top of our work.

That is not compatible with the OSI Open Source Definition (which requires
freedom 0 — use for any purpose including commercial). We deliberately step
outside "OSI open source" so we can be explicit about the intent:
**this is a public good, not a market opportunity**.
